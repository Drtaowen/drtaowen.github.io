Research interests

  • Complex network properties exploring by fractal property and entropy.

    • Network Properties: Combined Fuzzy theory and fractal dimension to evaluate the vulnerability of networks, which can describe nodes' relationships more precise; Considered the local structure property and whole structure property through Tsallis entropy to measure the complexity of networks.

    • Node Properties: Proposed several novel dimensions of nodes to identify influential individuals in the network; Modified the classical Kullback-Leibler divergence to consider more properties in network to find the pair of similar nodes.

    • Community Properties: Combined the internal factors and external factors of community to measure the vulnerability of each community via the entropy-based method; Proposed the gravity-based model to describe the large-scale interaction relationship between communities to further improve the quantification and identification ability of the vulnerability of communities.

  • Grope decision making based on game theory and network theory.

    • Collected the personal opinion of each expert by 2-order additive fuzzy measure, and identified the most important expert after constructing the expert network based on their relationships, thereby solving group decision-making problems without sufficient information.
